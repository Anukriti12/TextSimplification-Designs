Understanding Type 2 Diabetes

What Is Type 2 Diabetes?

Type 2 diabetes, the most common type of diabetes, is a disease that occurs when your blood glucose, also called blood sugar, is too high. Blood glucose is your main source of energy and comes mainly from the food you eat. Insulin, a hormone made by the pancreas, helps glucose get into your cells to be used for energy. In type 2 diabetes, your body does not make enough insulin or does not use insulin well. Too much glucose then stays in your blood, and not enough reaches your cells.

Health Problems Caused by Diabetes

Over time, having too much glucose in your blood can cause health problems such as heart disease, nerve damage, eye problems, and kidney disease. You are more likely to develop type 2 diabetes if you have a family history of diabetes, are overweight or have obesity, or are 35 or older. Many people with type 2 diabetes have no symptoms, or symptoms can be so mild that people might not even notice them.

Managing Your Diabetes

Managing your blood glucose, blood pressure, and cholesterol, and quitting smoking if you smoke, are important ways to manage your type 2 diabetes. Lifestyle changes that include planning healthy meals, limiting calories if you are overweight, and being physically active are also part of managing your diabetes. So is taking any prescribed medicines. Take your medicines as they were prescribed, even if you feel good or have reached your blood glucose, blood pressure, or cholesterol goals. Talk with your health care team if you cannot afford your medicines or if you experience any side effects.

Understanding Type 2 Diabetes

What Is Type 2 Diabetes?

Type 2 diabetes is a health condition where your blood sugar is too high. Blood sugar, also called glucose, comes from the food you eat. Your body uses a hormone called insulin to move sugar from your blood into your cells, where it is used for energy. In type 2 diabetes, your body does not make enough insulin or does not use it well. When this happens, too much sugar stays in your blood.

Health Problems Caused by Diabetes

If your blood sugar stays high for a long time, it can damage your heart, kidneys, eyes, and nerves. You are more likely to develop type 2 diabetes if you have a family member with diabetes, if you are overweight, or if you are 35 or older. Many people do not know they have diabetes because they feel no different at first.

Managing Your Diabetes

You can manage type 2 diabetes by eating healthy food, being physically active, and taking any medicines your doctor prescribes. Keep your blood sugar, blood pressure, and cholesterol at healthy levels. If you smoke, quitting will help. Take your medicines every day, even if you feel well. Do not stop taking them without talking to your doctor first. If your medicine is too expensive or causes side effects, tell your health care team.

Understanding Type 2 Diabetes

What Is Type 2 Diabetes?

Type 2 diabetes is a health condition. It means your blood sugar is too high. Blood sugar comes from the food you eat. Your body uses insulin to move blood sugar into your cells. In type 2 diabetes, insulin does not work well enough. Too much sugar stays in your blood.

Health Problems Caused by Diabetes

High blood sugar can damage your body over time. It can affect your heart, kidneys, eyes, and nerves. You are more likely to get diabetes if a family member has it. You are more likely to get it if you are overweight. Many people do not know they have it at first.

Managing Your Diabetes

Eat healthy food every day. Move your body regularly. Take your medicine every day, even if you feel well. Do not stop taking your medicine without asking your doctor. If your medicine costs too much or makes you feel unwell, tell your doctor or nurse.
