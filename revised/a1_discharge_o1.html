Hospital Discharge Instructions

Post-Operative Discharge Instructions

Rest and Activity

Rest today. An adult should stay with you the first night after your procedure. You may resume regular activities tomorrow. Driving: You may drive when you feel comfortable, usually within 24 hours following your procedure, unless otherwise instructed.

Pain Management

It is important that you stay ahead of the pain. Take pain medication as prescribed by your doctor. After 24 to 48 hours, you may take Acetaminophen (Tylenol) or Ibuprofen (Advil) for minor discomfort, unless you are restricted from using these. You should drink plenty of liquids and stick to a light diet for the next 24 hours.

Wound Care

Keep your dressing clean and dry. Remove plastic dressing, but leave steri strips on, in 36 to 48 hours. You may then take a shower. Do not submerge incision in a tub or pool. Wash your hands before and after replacing the band-aid. Call the office if you have increasing pain, fever greater than 101 degrees, increasing redness or drainage around the wound, nausea or vomiting that prevents you from taking medication or fluids, or any other concerns.

Hospital Discharge Instructions

Post-Operative Discharge Instructions

Rest and Activity

Rest at home today. Have an adult stay with you tonight. You can go back to regular activities tomorrow. You can usually drive within 24 hours, unless your doctor told you otherwise.

Pain Management

Take your pain medicine before the pain gets bad. After 24 to 48 hours, Tylenol or Ibuprofen is usually fine for mild pain, unless your doctor said no. Drink plenty of fluids. Eat light food for the first 24 hours.

Wound Care

Keep your wound dressing clean and dry. After 36 to 48 hours, remove the plastic part of the dressing but leave the small white strips on. You can shower after that. Do not soak the wound in a bathtub or pool. Always wash your hands before and after touching the wound. Call your doctor if you have pain that is getting worse, a fever above 101 degrees, redness or fluid at the wound, or if you feel too sick to take your medicine.

Hospital Discharge Instructions

Post-Operative Discharge Instructions

Rest and Activity

Rest at home today. Have someone stay with you tonight. You can go back to normal activities tomorrow. You can drive when you feel ready. This is usually the next day.

Pain Management

Take your pain medicine. Take it before the pain gets bad. Drink lots of water. Eat light food today.

Wound Care

Keep your wound covered and dry. After two days, remove the top plastic part. Leave the small white strips on. You can shower after two days. Do not sit in a bath or pool. Wash your hands before and after touching the wound. Call your doctor if your pain is getting worse. Call your doctor if you have a fever. Call your doctor if your wound looks red or has liquid coming out.
