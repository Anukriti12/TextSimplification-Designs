METFORMIN HCl 500 mg Tablets

What is GLUCOPHAGE used for?

GLUCOPHAGE (metformin hydrochloride) is a prescription medicine used to improve blood sugar (glucose) control in adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus. GLUCOPHAGE should be used with diet and exercise to improve blood sugar control. GLUCOPHAGE is not for people with type 1 diabetes.

Important Safety Information

GLUCOPHAGE and GLUCOPHAGE XR can cause a rare but serious condition called lactic acidosis (a build-up of lactic acid in the blood) that can cause death. Lactic acidosis is a medical emergency and must be treated in the hospital. Call your doctor right away if you have any of the following symptoms of lactic acidosis: feel very weak or tired; have unusual (not normal) muscle pain; have trouble breathing; have unexpected or unusual stomach discomfort; feel cold; feel dizzy or lightheaded; or have a slow or irregular heartbeat. Do not take GLUCOPHAGE or GLUCOPHAGE XR if your kidneys are not working properly.

How should I take GLUCOPHAGE?

Take GLUCOPHAGE exactly as your doctor tells you to take it. Take GLUCOPHAGE with meals. Your doctor may change your dose until your blood sugar is controlled. Do not change your dose unless your doctor tells you to. The usual starting dose of GLUCOPHAGE is 500 mg twice a day or 850 mg once a day. Common side effects of GLUCOPHAGE include diarrhea, nausea, and upset stomach. These side effects generally go away after you take the medicine for a while. Taking your medicine with meals can help reduce these side effects.

Prescription Medicine Label

METFORMIN HCl 500 mg Tablets

What is this medicine for?

Metformin is a prescription medicine that helps control your blood sugar if you have type 2 diabetes. It works best when you also eat healthy food and exercise regularly. Metformin is not used for type 1 diabetes.

Important warning

Metformin can cause a rare but serious problem called lactic acidosis. This is a build-up of acid in the blood and is a medical emergency. Call your doctor right away if you feel very weak or tired, have unusual muscle pain, have trouble breathing, feel cold, feel dizzy or lightheaded, or notice a slow or irregular heartbeat. Do not take this medicine if your kidneys are not working well.

How to take this medicine

Take Metformin exactly as your doctor prescribes. Always take it with food, at each meal. Do not change your dose unless your doctor tells you to. The usual starting dose is 500 mg twice a day. Common side effects include diarrhea, nausea, and upset stomach. These usually get better after a few weeks. Taking the medicine with food helps reduce stomach side effects.

Prescription Medicine Label

METFORMIN HCl 500 mg Tablets

What is this medicine for?

This medicine is called Metformin. It helps keep your blood sugar at a healthy level. It is for people with type 2 diabetes. You should also eat healthy food. You should also move your body regularly.

Important warning

Metformin can cause a serious problem. It is called lactic acidosis. Call your doctor right away if you feel very weak or tired. Call your doctor if your muscles hurt for no reason. Call your doctor if you have trouble breathing. Call your doctor if you feel dizzy or cold. Do not take this medicine if your kidneys are not working well.

How to take this medicine

Take one Metformin tablet with breakfast. Take one Metformin tablet with dinner. Always take it with food. Do not change how many tablets you take. Only your doctor can change your dose. You might feel sick to your stomach when you first start. This usually gets better after a few weeks.
