Health Insurance Letter

Explanation of Benefits

What is this document?

You may receive an EOB from your health plan after your visit with the provider. It will show you the total charges for your visit and how much you and your health plan owe. An EOB is NOT A BILL. You can also use it to track how you and your family use your coverage. You may get a separate bill from the provider.

Understanding Your Costs

Provider Charges is the amount your provider bills for your visit. Allowed Charges is the amount your provider will be paid; this may not be the same as the Provider Charges. Paid by Insurer is the amount your health plan will pay to your provider. What You Owe is the amount you owe after your insurer has paid everything else. You may have already paid part of this amount. A Remark Code is a note from the health plan that explains more about the costs, charges, and paid amounts for your visit.

Paying Your Bill and Appeals

Pay your bills and keep all paperwork in a safe place. Some providers will not see you if you have unpaid bills. You may be able to pay your bills online or over the phone. If you disagree with a coverage or payment decision by your health plan, you may be able to appeal. If you think you were charged for tests or services your coverage should pay for, keep the bill and call your health plan right away.

Health Insurance Letter

Explanation of Benefits

What is this document?

After you visit a doctor or hospital, your health plan sends you this letter. It is called an Explanation of Benefits, or EOB. This is not a bill. It shows what your visit cost, how much your health plan paid, and how much you might owe. Your doctor may send you a separate bill.

Understanding Your Costs

Provider Charges: the total amount your doctor billed. Allowed Charges: the amount your insurance agreed to pay for. Paid by Insurer: how much your insurance actually paid. What You Owe: the amount left for you to pay. There may also be a code on the letter. This code explains why your insurance paid less than the full amount.

Paying Your Bill and Appeals

Keep this letter and pay any bill you receive from your doctor. You can often pay online or by phone. If you think a decision is wrong, you have the right to appeal. Call the number on your insurance card to start an appeal.

Health Insurance Letter

Explanation of Benefits

What is this document?

This letter comes from your health insurance. It is called an Explanation of Benefits. This is not a bill. Do not pay money to the insurance company. It tells you what your visit cost. It tells you how much your insurance paid. It tells you how much you might owe to the doctor.

Understanding Your Costs

The letter shows four numbers. First: what your doctor charged. Second: what your insurance allows. Third: what your insurance paid. Fourth: what you owe. You pay the last number to your doctor's office.

Paying Your Bill and Appeals

Keep this letter in a safe place. Pay your doctor's bill when it arrives. You can usually pay online or by phone. If you think something is wrong, call the number on your insurance card. You have the right to ask them to look at it again.
