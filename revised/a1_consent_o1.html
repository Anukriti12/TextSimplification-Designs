Consent Form

Informed Consent to Surgical Procedure

What You Are Agreeing To

It is very important to your doctor that you understand and consent to the treatment your doctor is rendering and any surgery your doctor may perform. You should be involved in any and all decisions concerning the surgical procedure. Sign this form only after you understand the procedure, the risks, the alternatives, the risks associated with the alternatives, and all of your questions have been answered.

Risks of the Procedure

The risks and benefits associated with the procedure have been explained to me. However, I understand there is no certainty that I will achieve these benefits and no guarantee has been made to me regarding the outcome of the procedure. I also authorize the administration of sedation and/or anesthesia as may be deemed advisable or necessary for my comfort, well being and safety. The risks and possible undesirable consequences associated with the procedure have been explained to me including, but not limited to, blood loss, transfusion reactions, infection, heart complications, blood clots, loss of or loss of use of body part or other neurological injury or death.

Your Rights

In permitting my doctor to perform the procedure, I understand that unforeseen conditions may be revealed that may necessitate change or extension of the original procedure or a different procedure. The reasonable alternatives to the procedure have been explained to me. By signing below, I have had an opportunity to ask the doctor all questions concerning risks, alternatives, and risks of those alternatives.

Consent Form

Informed Consent to Surgical Procedure

What You Are Agreeing To

This form asks for your permission to have surgery or a medical procedure. Before you sign, make sure you understand what the procedure is, what the risks are, and what other options you have. Ask any questions you have. Only sign when you feel ready and understand everything.

Risks of the Procedure

Your doctor has explained the possible risks to you. These can include bleeding, infection, reactions to anaesthesia, blood clots, or other complications. In rare cases, more serious outcomes can occur. No doctor can guarantee a specific result. By signing, you agree to have anaesthesia or sedation if needed to keep you safe and comfortable.

Your Rights

During the procedure, your doctor may find something unexpected and may need to change the plan. You have been told about other options instead of this procedure. You have the right to ask questions about anything in this form before you sign. You can refuse treatment at any time.

Consent Form

Informed Consent to Surgical Procedure

What You Are Agreeing To

This form is asking if you agree to have a procedure or surgery. Ask questions if you do not understand. Only sign when you are ready. You should understand what will happen before you sign.

Risks of the Procedure

All procedures have some risks. Your doctor has explained the risks to you. Risks can include bleeding, infection, or reactions to medicine. No doctor can promise a specific result. By signing, you agree to medicine that will keep you comfortable during the procedure.

Your Rights

You have the right to say no to any part of this procedure. You can ask questions at any time. You can change your mind at any time. Your doctor will talk to you before making any big changes to the plan.
