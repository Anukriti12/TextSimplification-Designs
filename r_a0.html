Activity 0/Choose Your Document Type
Takes about 5 minutes. Participant picks the document type most relevant to their own life. This choice is used for all remaining activities.
CHI Fargo Health System Step 1 of 7

Which of these have you dealt with in real life?

We are going to look at five kinds of health documents people commonly receive. Pick the one that feels most familiar or most important to you. We will use that document for everything we do today.

💊
Prescription Label
Medicine Labels
Have you ever had to figure out how to take a medicine from the label on the bottle?
Labels tell you dose, timing, and warnings. Missing instructions can be dangerous.
🏥
Discharge Instructions
Hospital Discharge Papers
Have you ever gotten papers from a hospital telling you what to do when you go home?
These instructions tell you how to recover safely and when to call for help.
📄
Insurance Letter
Health Insurance Documents
Have you ever received a letter from your health insurance about a bill or what was covered?
These letters explain what was paid and what you still owe, and your rights to appeal.
✍️
Consent Form
Signing Before a Procedure
Have you ever been asked to sign a form agreeing to treatment at a hospital or clinic?
Consent forms are legal agreements. It is important to understand what you are signing.
📋
After-Visit Summary
Doctor Visit Summary
Have you ever gotten a printed summary of what happened during a doctor visit, with results and next steps?
These summaries remind you of test results and what to do before your next visit.
